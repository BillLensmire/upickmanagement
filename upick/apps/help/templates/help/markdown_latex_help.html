LaTeX Syntax

Inline Math

Use $...$ for inline math expressions.

The formula for water is $H_2O$.

Result: The formula for water is $H_2O$.

Display Math

Use $$...$$ for display math expressions.

$$E = mc^2$$

Result:

$$E = mc^2$$

Fractions

$\frac{numerator}{denominator}$

Example: $\frac{1}{2}$

Subscripts and Superscripts

$x^2$ (superscript)
$x_i$ (subscript)
$x_i^j$ (both)

Examples: $x^2$, $x_i$, $x_i^j$

Greek Letters

$\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta, \epsilon, \zeta, \eta, \theta$
$\iota, \kappa, \lambda, \mu, \nu, \xi, \pi, \rho$
$\sigma, \tau, \upsilon, \phi, \chi, \psi, \omega$
$\Gamma, \Delta, \Theta, \Lambda, \Xi, \Pi, \Sigma, \Phi, \Psi, \Omega$

Common Functions

$\sin(x), \cos(x), \tan(x)$
$\log(x), \ln(x), \exp(x)$
$\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$
$\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$
$\prod_{i=1}^{n} x_i$
$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) dx$

Matrices

$$\begin{pmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{pmatrix}$$

Result:

$$\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$$

Equations

$$\begin{align}
y &= mx + b \\
&= 2x + 3
\end{align}$$

Result:

$$\begin{align} y &= mx + b \\ &= 2x + 3 \end{align}$$
